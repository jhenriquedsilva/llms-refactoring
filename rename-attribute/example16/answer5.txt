**Analysis for Rename Attribute Refactoring Opportunities**

Reviewing the code, the prime candidates for “rename attribute refactoring” are places where field/variable/parameter names do not clearly reflect their meaning or usage, or are inconsistent. Here are a few observations:

- In the `DataClass` constructor: `boolean p2` does not match the field name `param2` and is ambiguous.
- The field names `param1`, `param2`, `param3` — while possibly okay in a minimal test, could be made more meaningful, but in the context of a generic test it can be reasonable to retain as is.
- The map keys match the attribute/parameter names, so aligning constructor args is important for clarity and contract.

**Best Practice**:
Rename `p2` to `param2` (to match field and semantic usage).
